LexiFlow Customer Care Business Rules

Sample policy document for RAG, customer care workflow, ticket creation, and image inspection demos.

1. Purpose

This document defines customer care rules for refund, replacement, warranty, technical support, escalation, image proof, and complaint ticket creation. LexiFlow should use these rules to answer customer questions and decide the correct next support action.

When a customer is eligible or eligible for review, the LexiFlow Customer Care Agent should offer to create a complaint ticket after customer confirmation. The agent should not simply tell the customer to contact support separately when LexiFlow can create the ticket.

2. Refund Policy

Customers may request a refund within 14 calendar days of delivery if the product is unused, undamaged, and returned with the original packaging.

If the customer is inside the 14 calendar day refund window, the customer care agent should mark the case as eligible for review and offer to create a complaint ticket.

If product condition is unknown, the customer care agent should request product image proof before creating or finalizing the complaint ticket.

Refund approval is not automatic. Final approval is completed after support review and warehouse inspection.

Refunds are not available after 14 calendar days of delivery unless an exception is approved by a human support manager.

3. Replacement Policy

A replacement may be offered within 30 calendar days of delivery if the product has a verified manufacturing defect.

If the customer is inside the 30 calendar day replacement window, the customer care agent should mark the case as eligible for review and offer to create a complaint ticket.

For replacement cases, the customer care agent should request product image proof when physical condition, damage, or defect evidence is relevant.

Replacement approval is subject to support review, product serial number validation, and defect verification.

4. Warranty Policy

Standard warranty covers manufacturing defects during the active warranty period. Warranty does not cover misuse, accidental drops, unauthorized repairs, liquid exposure, or normal wear and tear.

If the customer warranty is active and the issue may be covered, the customer care agent should offer to create a complaint ticket for support review.

Warranty claims may require proof of purchase, product serial number, and product image proof.

Active warranty alone does not require complaint ticket creation. If the customer only asks whether the warranty is active, what warranty covers, or whether the order is still under warranty, the customer care agent should answer the question and should not offer to create a complaint ticket unless the customer also reports a product issue or asks to raise a claim.

5. Technical Support Policy

If a product is not working, not turning on, or has a functional issue, the customer care agent should check the customer order, delivery age, warranty status, and relevant troubleshooting policy.

If the product is inside the warranty or replacement window, the customer care agent should offer to create a complaint ticket after customer confirmation.

For technical complaints, the technical support team will contact the customer within 4 working days.

The customer care agent should not promise immediate refund or replacement for technical cases without support review.

6. Image Proof and Damage Inspection

Product image proof is required or recommended for refund, replacement, warranty, technical, and delivery damage cases when product condition is relevant.

LexiFlow should pass the uploaded product image to the Image Detection Agent through the MCP damage inspection tool.

The Image Detection Agent should return inspection status, damage detected, damage type, severity, confidence, human review requirement, recommendation, image reference, and notes.

High severity damage, unclear images, or low confidence results should be routed to human review.

7. Complaint Ticket Rules

The customer care agent should create a complaint ticket only after the customer confirms that they want to proceed.

When the case requires image proof, the customer care agent should request the image before creating the complaint ticket.

Complaint tickets should be created only for active customer issues, claims, requests, or escalations. Do not create a complaint ticket for general policy questions, warranty-status questions, delivery-age questions, or informational questions when the customer has not reported a problem.

Ticket priority should be assigned as follows:

Issue TypePriorityReason
TechnicalHighFunctional issue may require specialist support.
ReplacementHighProduct defect must be reviewed quickly.
Delivery damageHighDamage during delivery requires urgent review.
RefundMediumEligibility and product condition must be checked.
WarrantyMediumCoverage and defect evidence must be verified.
BillingMediumPayment or invoice issue requires review.
GeneralLowInformational or low urgency request.

8. Escalation Rules

The customer care agent should escalate to human support when:

  • The policy document does not provide enough information.
  • The customer is outside the standard refund or replacement window but requests an exception.
  • The image inspection result is inconclusive.
  • The image inspection result shows high severity damage.
  • The customer reports repeated failures or safety concerns.
  • The customer disputes a previous support decision.

9. Customer-Facing Response Rules

Customer responses should be polite, concise, and practical. Responses should include customer name, order ID, delivery age, relevant policy window, eligibility status, and next action when these details are available.

The customer care agent should not invent policies, dates, approval status, warranty terms, or refund promises.

If the customer is eligible or eligible for review, the next action should be to offer complaint ticket creation inside LexiFlow.

If the customer is only asking for information and has not reported a product problem, refund request, replacement request, warranty claim, delivery issue, billing issue, or escalation request, the next action should be answer_only and no complaint ticket should be offered.
